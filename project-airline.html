Case Study

E-Commerce A/B Test Validation

Validating a new landing page design for a toy store platform by analyzing 400,000 web sessions with SQL and rigorous statistical methods to ensure UI/UX improvements effectively reduced user drop-off.

Tools Used

SQL, Python, Statistical Validation

Data Volume

400,000 Web Sessions

Project Focus

Bounce Rate Reduction

The Business Problem

The digital marketing team for a toy store e-commerce platform had a high bounce rate on their primary landing page. They hypothesized that a new, simplified UI/UX design would improve user engagement and reduce drop-off. My task was not just to compare the results, but to **rigorously validate** whether the new design ("Page B") was a *statistically significant* improvement over the old design ("Page A") before committing development resources to a full rollout.

My Analytical Approach

To provide a data-driven conclusion, I followed a two-step process to ensure the analysis was clean, accurate, and statistically sound.

Step 1: Data Cleaning & Filtering with SQL

The raw data contained over 400,000 web sessions, but not all were relevant to the A/B test. I used SQL to filter this traffic, isolating only the accurate test data. This involved:

  • Filtering for the specific timeframe of the test.
  • Isolating traffic that was correctly bucketed into "Page A" or "Page B".
  • Excluding internal traffic, bot traffic, and sessions with confounding variables (e.g., users who saw both versions).

Step 2: Rigorous Statistical Validation

Simply comparing the average bounce rates would be misleading. To confirm the results were not due to random chance, I conducted rigorous statistical validation. I used Python to:

  • Calculate the bounce rates for each group (Page A and Page B).
  • Perform a hypothesis test (e.g., a two-proportion z-test) to determine the p-value.
  • Calculate the **confidence intervals** for the difference in bounce rates, providing a clear range of the likely impact.

Findings & Deliverable

The analysis confirmed that the new design ("Page B") was a success. The results were not only positive but statistically significant.

My analysis showed that **Page B had a 3.5% lower bounce rate than Page A**. The hypothesis test resulted in a p-value of < 0.05, and the 95% confidence interval for the difference was [2.1%, 4.9%]. This provided high confidence that the observed improvement was real and not a statistical fluke.

Conclusion & Impact

I delivered a final report to stakeholders with a clear, data-driven conclusion: **Proceed with the redesigned page.**

By validating the UI/UX improvements with statistical rigor, I provided the team with the confidence needed to deploy the new design. This ensured development resources were spent effectively and directly contributed to a design that measurably reduced user drop-off, improving the top-of-funnel conversion path.
